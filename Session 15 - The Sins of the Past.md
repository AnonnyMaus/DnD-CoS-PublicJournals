# Session Overview

The party managed to save one child from the werewolf den, but a severe lycanthropy infection forces them to seek out Rudolph van Richten for a cure. With a ticking clock, they uncover ancient lore and infiltrate the ruined mansion of Argynvostholt, only to come face-to-face with the hateful undead commander, Vladimir Horngaard.

## The Narrative

### The Rescue & Secrets Revealed
After a grueling battle that proved nearly fatal for some, our heroes found themselves back outside the cavernous den. The one child they managed to save had been dragged away by a fleeing werewolf. Determined to rescue at least one innocent life, Borgür and Vinarius quickly tracked the beast's escape route. Once cornered, the werewolf held the terrified child hostage with a claw to its neck. Tildrak learned an important lesson about interpreting commands during the standoff, but thankfully Nirthar—whose sword is apparently magical or made of silver—was able to swiftly execute the beast and save the boy. 

The child—who introduced himself as "Little Jasper," son of Diedier (a pairing of names that felt strangely familiar)—was mostly in good health, save for a nasty neck wound that stubbornly refused to heal. 

While recovering in the woods, Ezmeralda finally opened up to the group, explaining her unhinged behavior in the den. She revealed that years ago, she and her mentor Rudolph van Richten came to Barovia to hunt Strahd, but the valley's oppressive dread eventually drove a wedge between them. During her time alone, she was attacked and contracted lycanthropy. Too proud to ask for help and terrified of becoming a creature of the night, she brutally amputated her own leg. During that chaotic fight, she lost a precious family heirloom—her last remaining link to her parents—which explained her frantic, desperate looting mid-battle. Seeing Borgür and Little Jasper suffering from the same infection, she insisted they seek out Rudolph immediately.

Prompted by Borgür's sudden craving for steak tartare, the heroes, Ezmeralda, and Little Jasper returned to Krezk to hand over the werewolf heads and the tragic bodies of the fallen children. The village was fairly grateful, but practically slammed the gates shut, refusing to offer the group shelter as long as they carried the lycanthropy infection. (The village had also already drank Vinarius's wine and "character motivation"). 

### Rudolph van Richten & Ancient Lore
After an uneventful night in a barn outside the city walls—save for a raven dropping by to say hello—the group traveled toward Lake Baratok. To pass the time on the road, Vinarius shared a bizarre story from his youth, detailing how he accidentally transformed into a dog and didn't know how to turn back, ending up living as a canine for an entire year. 

Upon reaching the tower, the party was met with a puzzle door. After executing a fancy little dance routine to bypass the locks, Rictavio finally opened up, dropping the disguise and revealing himself as the famed vampire hunter Rudolph van Richten. 

Examining Borgür and Little Jasper, Rudolph confirmed the severity of their infection. To cure them, they needed an ancient relic. He laid out three distinct scraps of lore across his table:

**1. A brittle, blood-stained page from the Book of Vladimir (a silver knight's diary):**
> *"The battle is lost before it begins. Lord Argynvost commands us to hold the line while the warrior-priestess, Lugdana, retreats to the high vault. She carries exactly what the Devil seeks to destroy. She must seal it away in stone before she joins us in the slaughter."*

**2. An unnerving, old Barovian nursery rhyme the Vistani sing:**
> *"When the silver wing fell from the sky, the valley wept. Only the black feathers remained to guard the bones, hiding the sun's tear where the mountain sleeps."*

**3. A charred fragment from the personal research notes of Lugdana, the Saint of Ravens:**
> *"My Lord Strahd grows impatient with my ravens; he demands soldiers who can slaughter, not just observe. His influence corrupts my mind, yet I obey. Using the sun's tear, I have twisted the blood of men, forging wolves of shadow. What have I done? Only the relic that forged this curse can unmake it. I must secret it away at the silver dragon's house before my Lord realizes its power to undo his new army."*

Piecing these cryptic fragments together, the party deduced an uncomfortable truth: the mysterious relic hidden in the fallen house of Argynvostholt—referred to in the texts as "The Sun's Tear"—must be the key to unmaking the corrupted wolf strain. Rudolph alluded that this relic could be the *only* thing capable of curing Borgür and Little Jasper. To get it, however, they would need to infiltrate a ruin filled with undead revenants. Rudolph revealed he had already attempted to secure the relic himself, but was driven from the ruins when he proudly admitted to the commander that he was a vampire hunter intent on killing Strahd. 

### A Journey to the Fallen House
Leaving Ezmeralda and Little Jasper behind in the safety of the tower, the heroes wasted no time and prepared to brave the mountains. To aid their search for a way to defeat the Devil, Rudolph handed them a crumbling, bark-bound journal before they left, tasking them with reading it on the road. 

As they traveled south toward the mansion, they examined the **Druid's Journal**, finding a terrifying first-hand account of how Strahd draws his invulnerability from the land itself:

> *"Before the Outlander came to this valley, the lands belonged to the Ladies Three. The Huntress, the Weaver, and the Seeker. They were the Land, and we were its children. We knew not of lords or kings, only the beating heart of the earth.*
> 
> *But the Outlander did not just conquer the people; he conquered the very stones. He sought out our ancient monuments to bind the Fanes to his own dark soul. I was there when the Mountain Fane fell. I still hear the screams.*
> 
> *He dragged our elders to the standing stones under a starless sky. He demanded we sing the sacred rites to summon the Huntress. When the high druid spat at his feet and refused to betray our Mother, the Outlander did not even draw his blade. He simply snapped the old man's neck with one pale hand and tossed him into the sacred fires. He looked at the rest of us, his eyes burning like red coals in the dark, and told us to sing, or burn with him.*
> 
> *We sang. We wept, and we sang the ancient, beautiful songs of our people, knowing our cowardice was a betrayal. The Huntress felt the malice in the air. The winds shifted; she knew it was a trap. But she is bound by the primal laws of the valley. When her children cry out in the sacred tongue, she must answer.*
> 
> *The moment she manifested, glowing with the fierce spirit of the peaks, the Outlander struck. He did not fight her—he polluted her. He drove a black iron spike through the heart of the altar and poured his own cursed, undead blood over the stones. The Huntress shrieked as her light turned a wretched, sickly crimson. He chained her essence to his own.* 
> 
> *He did this to all Three. By desecrating the Fanes, he stole their domain. He became the Land.*
> 
> *So long as his dark heart beats, the Three are imprisoned, their primal magic siphoned to fuel his invulnerability. The peasants think they can slay him with swords, but he cannot die while he holds the earth hostage. To slay the devil, one must first break his grip on the Land, piece by piece. For my goddess of the Mountain, her chains must be burned away by the sacred power of the sun. As for her sisters, I know not what relics can break their ancient fetters. I only hope someone finds them before the valley is consumed entirely."*

### The Commander's Wrath
They safely scaled the peak toward the mansion without issue. Upon arrival, two things were immediately apparent: the castle was a ruined shell of its former glory, and a massive dragon statue stood watch, bearing the name "Argynvost." 

The party cautiously explored the darkened halls. They briefly interacted with an undead knight named Sir Godfrey Gwilym, but quickly moved on, dismissing him as largely unhelpful. After surviving a fleeting confrontation with some invisible poltergeist furniture, they boldly stepped into the throne room.

The moment they crossed the threshold, the massive undead commander—Vladimir Horngaard—rose from his throne. Screaming with an unparalleled hatred, he furiously declared that anyone attempting to defeat Strahd was an enemy, for the vampire must never be granted the mercy of death. He was to suffer in this misty prison eternally as punishment! Fixating on the heavily armed party, he roared a final, chilling threat: 

> *"If you have come to destroy me, know this: I perished defending this land from evil over four centuries ago, and because of my failure, I am forever doomed. If you destroy this body, my spirit will find a new corpse to inhabit, and I will hunt you down. You cannot free me from my damnation, nor would I wish it."*

## The Facts
* **The Rescue:** Saved "Little Jasper" but discovered he is similarly infected with the corrupted lycanthropy strain alongside Borgür.
* **Denied Entry:** Krezk returned the children's bodies and rejected the party's request for shelter due to the lycanthropy threat.
* **Ezmeralda's History:** The party learned Ezmeralda amputated her own leg to stop lycanthropy from spreading in her blood. The heirloom she frantically searched for was a memento of her parents lost during that werewolf attack.
* **The Cure:** Rudolph van Richten (no longer disguised) revealed the only cure for the corrupted werewolf strain is the Holy Symbol of Ravenkind ("The Sun's Tear"), located in Argynvostholt.
* **Strahd's Fanes:** The party learned via a Druid's Journal that Strahd's invulnerability is tied to the desecration of the Three Fanes, and they must use the sacred power of the sun to free the Huntress.
* **The Commander:** The party infiltrated Argynvostholt, dismissed Sir Godfrey, and directly provoked the hostile revenant commander, Vladimir Horngaard.
